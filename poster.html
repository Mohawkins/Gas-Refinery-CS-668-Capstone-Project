Gasoline Prices Increase: CS-668 Capstone Project

Mohawkins | CS-668 Capstone Project

1. Introduction

This project investigates the multifaceted factors contributing to the volatility and upward trend in gasoline prices. We analyze historical data, geopolitical events, and supply chain dynamics to understand the primary drivers. The goal is to develop a predictive model for short-term price fluctuations and provide insights for consumers and policymakers.

2. Methodology

Our approach involves a blend of data analysis and theoretical framework application:

  1. Data Collection: Gathering weekly national average price data, crude oil prices (WTI/Brent), and global stock change indicators from 2010 to present.
  2. Model Selection: Utilizing Time Series Analysis (ARIMA/Prophet) for forecasting short-term price movements.
  3. Feature Engineering: Incorporating external variables like seasonality, major oil-producing nation policy changes, and consumer demand indices.
  4. Behavioral Analysis: Considering consumer behavior and market perception, as illustrated in our theoretical framework.
Figure 2: Theoretical framework and research hypothesis for analyzing energy consumption behavior.

3. Key Results & Findings

The analysis shows a strong correlation ($R^2 > 0.85$) between crude oil futures and domestic pump prices with a 2-week lag. Furthermore, our model predicts a 5% average increase in prices over the next quarter, primarily driven by anticipated OPEC+ production cuts and rising summer demand.

4. Discussion & Impact

Our findings underscore the sensitivity of gasoline prices to both global supply factors and local demand shocks. Events such as hurricanes can lead to immediate and significant price spikes due to disruptions in refining and distribution, often exacerbated by panic buying.

5. Conclusion & Future Work

Gasoline price volatility remains a significant economic and social factor. Our model provides a robust foundation for prediction, but integrating real-time API data and exploring advanced machine learning techniques (e.g., LSTM networks) will be crucial for improving long-term accuracy and understanding localized impacts.

Further research will also delve into the effectiveness of policy interventions designed to mitigate price shocks and promote energy stability.
